About

Hello there, welcome to my personal portfolio.

I'm Kyle, a Stellenbosch University Engineering student. I'm currently doing my masters in autonomous robotics in the Electronic Systems Laboratory (ESL).

I have a deep passion for robotics as I have been an RC lover from a young age. During my academic career I've had the privilege of developing some really cool and functional platforms, such as a servo motor biped that used trajectory optimisation to generate walking gaits and a RHex-styled platform that features a tethered drone for use in improved navigation and path planning.

When I'm not conducting groundbreaking research I am often tinkering with my 3D printers or trying to break the sound barrier with an RC quadcopter.

Publications

Publications stemming from my research

Tethered Multi-Robot System for Improved Path Planning and Exploration

This paper details the process of designing a multi-robot system comprising a RHex-styled rover and tethered drone. The specific algorithms and processes followed to enable the platform to successfully navigate and deploy the drone to achieve an aerial view are discussed. The efficacy of the additive bird’s-eye-view, that the drone supplies in the path planning process, was evaluated for contributing to the system's ability to navigate complex, unknown terrain(s).
Accepted into the 2023 iteration of ICAR - International Conference on Advanced Robotics.
Selected for presentation and panel discussion (Multi-Robot Systems) at ICAR 2023.

Using a Multi-Robot System for Improved Path Planning

The main objective of this paper was to evaluate the viability of using a tethered drone to assist a ground-based rover in navigating environments wherein the rover would be unable to see over certain obstacles, due to being a low-to-ground platform.
Published in 2023 RAPDASA-RobMech-PRASA-AMI Conference.
