RHex Robot with Tethered Drone

This project formed the basis of my masters research. The aim of the project was to develop a multi-robot system, consisting of a ground-based RHex-styled platform and accompanying drone. The RHex makes use of both cameras and a LiDAR sensor for navigation and object detection, like most traditional exploration robots. These sensors on the RHex are subject to the height constraints of the platform, due to portability requirements, and as such are unable to perceive the environment beyond the line-of-sight of the RHex, which can limit the platform's ability to perform effecient path planning.

For this research, an aerial drone was added to the RHex platform to allow for an on-demand/as-needed bird's-eye-view to improve the system's path planning ability. Taking into account the standard short flight time of typical drones, the drone was tethered to the chassis of the RHex and carried as a payload when an aerial view was not needed by the autonomous system or not requested by the handler. This approach served to reap the benefits of both ground- and air-based autonomous vehicles. For this, both the RHex-styled platform and the FPV drone were custom built and programmed to serve their unique purpose.

This project was immensely challenging and required becoming familiar with computer vision, autonomous robotics, LiDAR data processing, multi-threading, Object-oriented programming (OOP), modular programming and distributed control systems.
